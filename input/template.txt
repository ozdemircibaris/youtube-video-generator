#content: Cartoon Dreams
Emma loves cartoons. She watches them every day after school. Her favorite cartoon is about a cat and a mouse. They are funny and make her happy.

Emma wants to make her own cartoon. She draws many pictures in her notebook. Her mom says she is good at drawing.

At school, Emma tells her friends about her cartoon ideas. Some friends laugh at her.

One friend says, Emma, cartoons are hard to make.

But Emma does not stop. She draws more and more every day.

One day, a new art teacher comes to school. His name is Mr. Tom. He sees Emma drawing during lunch.

Can I see your pictures? asks Mr. Tom.

Emma shows him her notebook. It has many colorful drawings.

These are very good, says Mr. Tom. Do you like cartoons?

Yes, I love them! Emma says with a big smile.

Mr. Tom tells Emma a secret. He worked on a famous cartoon show before becoming a teacher.

I can teach you how to make simple animations, he says.

Every Monday after school, Emma learns from Mr. Tom. He shows her how to make pictures move.

After three months, Emma makes a short cartoon about a girl and her cat. It is only one minute long, but she is very proud.

The school has a small art show. Emma shows her cartoon. All the students and teachers watch it.

Everyone claps for Emma. Even the friends who laughed before.

Mr. Tom smiles at Emma. You did it! Your first cartoon!

Emma is very happy. She knows this is just the start of her cartoon dreams.

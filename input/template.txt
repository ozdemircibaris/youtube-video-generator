#content: The Missing Backpack
Leo was excited. It was Saturday, and the weather was perfect. He packed his backpack with snacks, water, and his favorite book. He was going to the park to relax and read under a big tree.

At the park, children were playing, people were walking their dogs, and birds flew across the blue sky. Leo smiled. He found a quiet spot under a tree and sat down.

He opened his book and started reading. After some time, he ate his snacks and drank some water. Then, he lay down and closed his eyes for a short nap.

When Leo woke up, the sun was lower in the sky. He stretched his arms and looked around—but his backpack was gone!

He stood up quickly. “Where’s my backpack?” he said out loud.

Leo walked around the park, looking under benches and near trees. He asked a few people, “Have you seen a blue backpack?” But no one had seen it.

He felt worried. His book wasn’t the problem—but his house key was inside the backpack!

Just then, a small voice said, “Are you looking for this?” A young girl was standing nearby, holding his backpack.

“Yes!” Leo said. “That’s mine! Thank you so much!”

“I found it near the tree,” the girl said. “I waited because I thought the owner would come back.”

“You’re very kind,” Leo smiled. “What’s your name?”

“I’m Mia,” she said.

Leo thanked Mia again and offered her one of his cookies from the backpack. She smiled and took one.

“You’re welcome,” Mia said. “My mom always says: ‘If you find something, try to help.’”

“That’s great advice,” Leo said. “You really helped me today.”

They talked a little more, then Mia went back to her family. Leo sat down again, this time holding his backpack close.

He looked at the sunset and felt happy. Not just because he found his backpack, but because he met someone nice.

#content: Basic English Sentences for Everyday Life
Sarah is new to the city. Today is her first English class.

The teacher starts with simple greetings. "Good morning! How are you today?"

Sarah practices the response. "I am fine, thank you. And you?"

The class learns more greetings. "Hello. Hi. Good afternoon. Good evening."

Next, they practice introductions. "My name is Sarah. Nice to meet you."

The teacher explains questions. "What is your name? Where are you from? How old are you?"

After class, Sarah goes to a café to practice.

"Excuse me, can I have a coffee, please?" she asks.

"Would you like small, medium, or large?" asks the barista.

"Medium, please. How much is it?" Sarah replies.

"That will be three dollars," says the barista.

Sarah pays and says, "Thank you very much."

"You're welcome. Have a nice day!" the barista responds.

The next day, Sarah goes shopping at the supermarket.

"Excuse me, where is the bread?" she asks a worker.

"The bread is in aisle five," the worker answers.

Sarah finds what she needs and goes to pay. "Do you have a loyalty card?"

"No, I don't. How can I get one?" Sarah asks.

Later, Sarah meets her neighbor. "Hello, my name is Sarah. I just moved here."

"Hi Sarah, I'm Emma. Where are you from?" asks Emma.

"I'm from Spain. I moved here for work," Sarah explains.

"How do you like it so far?" Emma asks.

"I like it very much. The people are friendly," Sarah replies.

On the weekend, Sarah gets lost and needs directions.

"Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to the library?" she asks.

"Go straight for two blocks, then turn right at the traffic light," a person explains.

"Is it far from here?" Sarah asks.

"No, it's about a ten-minute walk," they reply.

At the library, Sarah asks, "Do you have books for learning English? Can I borrow this book?"

"Your book is due back in two weeks," says the librarian.

After one month, Sarah can handle basic conversations in many situations.

"Practice makes perfect," her teacher always says. Sarah agrees.
